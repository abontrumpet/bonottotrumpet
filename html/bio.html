Bio

Alessandro Bonotto is a musician with the United States Naval Forces Europe Band and in the final stages of his doctoral degree at Michigan State University.

Just before winning his job, Alessandro was Adjunct Professor of Trumpet at Saginaw Valley State University as well as the trumpet instructor at the Flint Institute of Music. As an educator, he has given several masterclasses to local middle and high schools. Additionally, he has given masterclasses as part of the faculty Beaumont Brass Quintet (MSU) at college level institutions such as University of Akron, Youngstown State University, Michigan State University, Montclair State University, The Juilliard School, and University of Wisconsin-Madison.

Alessandro attended MSU as a graduate assistant and taught lessons to undergraduate students. Prior to MSU, Alessandro attended Indiana University where he was the trumpet graduate assistant and a member of the Associate Instructor Quintet. He also attended the University of Oklahoma and University of Central Florida where and his primary teachers included Justin Emerich, John Rommel, Karl Sievers, and John Almeida. The summer before entering the Navy, Alessandro spent the summer at the Aspen Music Festival where he studied with Ray Mase, Kevin Cobb, and Jim Wilt. In addition to AMF, he received full scholarships to attend Brevard Music Festival, Marrowstone Music Festival, and Domaine Forget.

As an orchestral musician, Alessandro performed frequently with the Fort Wayne Philharmonic and the Flint Symphony Orchestra. Additionally he has performed with a number of regional orchestras including Oklahoma City Philharmonic and Richmond (IN) Symphony.

Alessandro enjoys cycling, eating, and traveling with his wife Anastasia.
