BIOGRAPHY

Alessandro Bonotto is a musician with the United States Naval Forces Europe Band and in the final stages of his doctoral degree at Michigan State University.

Prior to joining the US Navy Fleet Bands, Alessandro was an active educator in the Greater Lansing area where he was adjunct professor of trumpet at Saginaw Valley State University and was the trumpet instructor at Flint Institute of Music. He frequently gave masterclasses at local middle and high schools and has also given masterclasses at institutions such as University of Akron, Youngstown State University, Michigan State University, Montclair State University, The Juilliard School, and University of Wisconsin-Madison, as part of the faculty Beaumont Brass Quintet (MSU).

For his Doctoral studies, Alessandro attended MSU and Indiana University. While at MSU he was a trumpet graduate assistant and his duties included playing with the faculty brass quintet, playing in the orchestra and wind symphony, teaching trumpet methods, and teaching lessons to undergraduates. At IU he was the trumpet graduate assistant where he played in the associate-instructor quintet and assisted his professor, John Rommel, with various tasks. He took advantage of the variety of opportunities at IU and gave numerous world premieres for student composers, performed in the New Music Ensemble under David Dzubay, was in the conductorâ€™s orchestra, and performed in ensembles. For his Masters, Alessandro attended University of Oklahoma where he studied with Karl Sievers and for his undergraduate degree he went to the University of Central Florida with John Almeida.

Alessandro has enjoyed a varied performing career mainly as an orchestral musician. In the summer of 2018, he attended the Aspen Music Festival where his primary teachers were Ray Mase, Kevin Cobb, and Jim Wilt. In 2017 he attended Brevard Music Center as a teaching assistant and in years prior he attended Marrowstone Music Festival as a fellow, and Domain Forget on their maximum scholarship in which he participated in the final faculty concert. Alessandro performed frequently with Fort Wayne Philharmonic, Flint Symphony, Oklahoma City Philharmonic, and various others. Other performing engagements have included part of the national tour of Chicago The Musical, the Brass Band of Central Florida, and the Sea World Brass Quintet.

Alessandro enjoys cycling around the greater Napoli area, trying different Neapolitan pizza places, and travelling with his wife, Anastasia
